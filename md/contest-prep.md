# Contest Prep Protocols — Eric Zunkley
*Based on: J3U Future of Contest Prep, Contest Prep Decision-Making, Clenbuterol, Offseason Periodization PDFs*

---

## Prep Timeline Framework

**NOT a fixed 12–16 week prep.** Personalize based on visual assessment:
- Compare current photos to stage-ready reference photos
- Assess how much fat needs to go and at what realistic rate

**Fat loss rate targets:**
- Early prep (higher BF): ~1.5% of body weight/week (~1–1.5 lbs/week)
- Late prep (getting lean): ~0.5% body weight/week (~0.5–1 lb/week)
- As leanness increases, the rate MUST slow to protect muscle

**Pre-prep tip:** Spend a few weeks at end of offseason gathering weekly visual data before starting the prep clock. Discovering mid-prep that you underestimated starting BF is a common failure point.

**Example 22-week timeline (230 lb starting, 35 lbs to lose):**
- Weeks 1–10: ~1.5% body weight/week loss (~3.4 lbs/wk) — higher BF allows steeper deficit
- Weeks 11–20: ~0.5% body weight/week (~1–1.5 lbs) — lean enough that preservation is priority
- Weeks 21–22: Peak week — raise food, reduce fatigue, practice posing. No more fat loss.

## Fat Loss Stall Decision Tree

**Step 1:** Confirm >7–14 days of strict adherence before changing anything.

**Step 2:** Are you visually leaner AND scale/calipers moving?
- Yes, within 0.5–1.5% body weight/week → **no change needed**
- Yes, but stalled → check confounders:
  - Poor sleep or high stress (cortisol = water retention)
  - Water retention from increased training load
  - GI/digestion changes
  - Different time of weigh-in
  - Change in daily routine or food volume
- No visual change at all → see Step 3

**Step 3:** Is there room for further deficit (calories not bottomed out, cardio not maxed)?
- **Yes** → Fat Loss Action Step 1 (calories)
- **No** → Is fatigue high? Yes → Fatigue Action Step 1 (diet break + deload); No → Fat Loss Action Step 2 (cardio)

## Fat Loss Action Steps

### Step 1 — Calories
One change at a time. Options:
- 10% total kcal reduction (e.g., 200 kcal off a 2,000 kcal diet)
- Or calculate: target 0.5 lb/week loss = ~240 kcal/day deficit

Macro reduction priority order:
1. Decrease carbs if: carbs >1g/lb AND fat <0.3g/lb (protect pre/intra/post-workout carbs last)
2. Decrease fat if: carbs <1g/lb AND fat >0.7g/lb (trace fats OK short-term in late prep)
3. Decrease protein (last) if: protein >1.3g/lb, carbs <1g/lb, fat <0.3g/lb

**Late-prep performance hack:** If no calorie cut is needed but gym performance is falling — lower protein to 1g/lb, redistribute those calories as carbohydrate.

### Step 2 — Energy Expenditure (Cardio)
Add in this order, minimizing fatigue impact:
1. Add 2–4k steps/day
2. Add 10–15 min/session to moderate cardio OR add 1–2 sessions/week
3. Convert NEAT to structured cardio
- **Rule:** Cardio sessions should not exceed number of weight training sessions

### Step 3 — Fat Loss Agents
Use "get the most from the least" — do NOT add all at once at top dose:
- **T3 (Cytomel):** Get thyroid labs at mid-prep; start 15–25mcg/day based on labs
- **Clenbuterol:** Start 20mcg AM; titrate up every 3–7 days; max 80mcg/day; stop 3 days before show
- **Yohimbine HCl:** Start 2.5mg/day; titrate to 0.2mg/kg body weight
- **Caffeine:** Start 100mg/day; increase to 200–300mg/day

## Muscle Retention Action Steps

### Step 1 — Training Adjustments
Fight to maintain load and reps. Autoregulate rather than drop weight:
- Remove metabolic techniques first: supersets, rest-pause, forced reps, dropsets
- Reduce work set count per session
- Split volume into more sessions with shorter duration:
  - PPL → Push/Pull/Delt+Arms/Legs
  - PPL → Upper/Lower
- Progress to lower-fatigue exercises as prep deepens:
  - Early: Lighter deadlifts or good mornings / Late: Machine good morning / Final: Back extension
  - (Heavy deadlifts are not part of Eric's program — lighter DL or good mornings are the starting point)

### Step 2 — PED Adjustment (last 4–6 weeks, last resort)
Add one compound at a time:
- Oxandrolone (Anavar): 20mg, last 4–8 weeks, 1hr pre-training with 300mg caffeine
- Stanozolol: 25mg, last 4–6 weeks
- Halotestin: 20mg, last 2–3 weeks ONLY (very short window)

## Fatigue Management

### Diet Break (7–14 days, for high accumulated fatigue)
- Raise calories to maintenance (20–30% increase) via carbohydrates
- Protein and fat unchanged; same food sources
- Training: cut work sets 50%, same exercises, same loads, train to 1 RIR
- Cardio stays the same unless fatigue is severe

### Refeed (24–72 hours, short-term reset)
- Raise calories 20–30% via carbohydrates only
- Protein and fat same or slightly reduced; offset with carbs
- Training and cardio unchanged
- If leg fatigue is very high: steps only during refeed

## Clenbuterol — Protocol Detail

**Mechanism:** Beta-2 agonist → adenylyl cyclase → cAMP → PKA → PPAR-alpha + UCP1 → increased fatty acid oxidation. Also decreases protein degradation in skeletal muscle.

**Key insight:** Skeletal muscle B2 receptors downregulate after ~18 days (tremors resolve, muscle anabolic effect diminishes). BUT adipose tissue blood flow INCREASES 5-fold — fat-loss benefit continues chronically even as tolerance develops to the muscle effects.

**Protocol:**
- 34-hour half-life → single daily morning dose
- Start: 20mcg AM → titrate every 3–7 days → max 80mcg/day
- Stop 3 days before show (allows sleep and fatigue recovery for peak)
- Implement AFTER calories are already low and cardio is already structured — stimulants disrupt sleep, which impairs recovery

---

## Peak Week
- Weeks 21–22 in a long prep (or final 2 weeks)
- Raise food intake
- Reduce training fatigue
- Practice posing extensively
- No more aggressive fat loss — body is being primed, not depleted
