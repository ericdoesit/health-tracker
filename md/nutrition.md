## Phase 1 — Cut
**Goal:** 179 lb → 167 lb at ~10% BF | Rate: ~0.9 lb/week | Duration: ~12 weeks

| Macro | Daily | Training Day | Rest Day |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| Calories | 2,100 | 2,100 | 2,080 |
| Protein | 215g | 215g | 215g |
| Carbs | 195g | 225g | 160g |
| Fat | 65g | 50g | 75g |

**Rationale:**
- Protein at 1.2g/lb — evidence-based upper range for lean mass retention on a cut. Goes no higher because beyond 1.2g/lb shows diminishing returns and displaces carbs needed for training performance.
- Fat minimum 0.36g/lb (65g) — floor required to maintain testosterone and hormone production. Going lower on a cut risks hormonal suppression.
- Carbs fill remaining calories. Higher on training days (carb cycling) to fuel performance and spare protein. Lower on rest days.
- Deficit: ~400 cal/day = ~0.8–1.0 lb/week loss at 2,500 TDEE.

**Lean mass protection rules:**
- If losing >1.5 lb/week for 2+ weeks → add 100 cal (raise carbs, not fat)
- If strength drops >10% on main lifts → cut cardio before cutting calories
- Deload every 4th week: same weight, 40% less volume

**Tirzepatide note:** GLP-1 strongly suppresses hunger. Risk is eating well below 2,100 without realizing it. Log every meal in the app to confirm you're actually hitting the target — not just feeling full.

## Phase 2 — Transition
**Goal:** Metabolic reset, deload, move from cut to bulk | Duration: 2 weeks

| Macro | Daily | Training Day | Rest Day |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| Calories | 2,500 | 2,500 | 2,480 |
| Protein | 200g | 200g | 200g |
| Carbs | 275g | 305g | 240g |
| Fat | 75g | 65g | 85g |

**Rationale:**
- Calories return to maintenance (TDEE ~2,500). No deficit, no surplus.
- Protein drops slightly to 200g (1.2g/lb at new ~167 lb goal weight) — muscle is no longer under threat of deficit.
- Carbs increase significantly to replenish muscle glycogen depleted during the cut.
- Fat increases slightly on rest days to support hormone recovery.
- Training volume drops ~40% for 2 weeks (deload). This is intentional — don't fight it.

**Key purpose:** Allow leptin, thyroid, and cortisol to normalize after 12 weeks of deficit. Skipping this phase increases fat gain rate in the subsequent bulk.

## Phase 3 — Lean Bulk
**Goal:** Build muscle at 167 lb starting weight | Rate: 0.25–0.5 lb/week | Duration: 20–24 weeks

| Macro | Daily | Training Day | Rest Day |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| Calories | 2,800 | 2,800 | 2,760 |
| Protein | 185g | 185g | 185g |
| Carbs | 362g | 410g | 310g |
| Fat | 65g | 55g | 75g |

**Rationale:**
- Surplus: ~300 cal/day above maintenance. Slow surplus minimizes fat gain. At 0.25–0.5 lb/week gain rate, expect ~80–90% of weight gained to be muscle.
- Protein at 1.1g/lb (185g at 167 lb) — sufficient for hypertrophy. Higher protein isn't needed in a surplus since carbs spare protein more effectively.
- Carbs are highest here — carbs are anabolic and the primary fuel for hypertrophy training. Higher carbs = better insulin response = better nutrient partitioning.
- Fat stays at the hormone floor (65g, 0.39g/lb). No benefit to raising fat in a bulk when carbs are more useful.
- Training day carbs much higher (410g) to fuel volume, support glycogen, and maximize anabolic response.

**Bulk exit triggers:**
- BF% reaches 15–17% → return to cut phase
- Target body weight at end of bulk: ~175–180 lb at 14–15% BF

## Meal Structure (all phases)

**4–5 meals per day, spaced 3–4 hours apart**

Each meal must contain **43–54g protein** to maximally stimulate muscle protein synthesis (MPS). Below ~40g per meal under-stimulates MPS. Above ~60g per meal, the excess is oxidized for energy rather than used for muscle.

| Timing | Carb Type | Fat | Notes |
|--------|-----------|-----|-------|
| Pre-workout (1–2 hrs before) | Simple (fast-digesting) | Minimize | Maximize energy availability |
| Post-workout (within 2 hrs) | Simple + complex | Low | Replenish glycogen, spike insulin |
| Rest of meals | Complex | Moderate | Sustained energy, hormone support |

**Daily non-negotiables:**
- 100–130 oz water (more if exercising or on tirzepatide)
- Creatine: 5g/day with any meal (no loading needed; timing doesn't matter)
- Leucine: naturally hit if protein comes from high-quality sources (chicken, eggs, whey, fish, beef)

---

## Protein Source Hierarchy
1. **Whey protein** — fastest absorption, highest leucine content (~2.7g/serving)
2. **Chicken breast / turkey** — lean, ~30g per 4 oz
3. **Eggs / egg whites** — high bioavailability
4. **Fish (salmon, tuna, cod)** — protein + omega-3s (anti-inflammatory)
5. **Greek yogurt (Fage 2%)** — protein + probiotics
6. **Beef (lean cuts)** — creatine + zinc + iron
7. **Cottage cheese** — casein (slow-digesting, good before bed)

## Calorie Adjustment Protocol
Track weekly average weight (not daily). Adjust if needed:

| Situation | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Losing >1.5 lb/wk for 2 weeks (cut) | +100 cal (add carbs) |
| Losing <0.5 lb/wk for 2 weeks (cut) | −100 cal (remove carbs) |
| Gaining >0.75 lb/wk for 2 weeks (bulk) | −100 cal (remove carbs) |
| Gaining <0.15 lb/wk for 2 weeks (bulk) | +100 cal (add carbs) |
| Strength drops >10% | Reduce cardio before cutting calories |

## BIA Scale Notes (Withings)
The scale measures lean mass via bioelectrical impedance, which is highly sensitive to hydration. Rules for consistent readings:
- Always weigh **morning, fasted, after drinking 16 oz water**
- Do not weigh after alcohol, heavy sodium, or intense exercise
- A 152→148 lb "lean mass" swing is likely dehydration, not actual muscle loss
- Use 2–4 week trends, not individual readings
- Lifts declining = more reliable signal of muscle loss than scale readings
